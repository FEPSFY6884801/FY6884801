Grover's Algorithim
 
      Grover's alogrithim is designed to search an unsorted database or solving problems with much greater efficiency than classical algorithims (Figgatt et al., 2017).
      It can be used to search through an unstructred database containing N items to find a specific item that satisfies the condition. What makes it stand out to 
      classical alogorithims is they tend to require an average of O(N) evaluations to find the key item but Grover's can do it in O(N^1/2) evaluations. This 
      is a significant quadratci speed up and is possible because it utilises quantum superposition allowing multiple evalutions simulataneously. While it does
      not directly predict outcomes, in predicitve analysis it can identify the most impactful features in datasets used for stock price predictions. It can
      also be used for alogorthimic trading or HFT (high frequency trading) as it cna ehance trading strategies by quickly searching for the best trading signals.

     
Shor's Algorithim

      Shor's alogorithim is designed for integer facorisation, which is the process of decompsoing a composite number into its prime factors. This specific 
      alogrithim has significant implications for systems that rely on teh difficulty of ffactoring large numbers. The genral number field sieve, which is a
      classical algorithim for factorising, operates in exponential time whereas Shor's can factoring is polynominal time (O((LogN)^3))). Not only does it use 
      superposition and entanglemt, allowing multiple possibilites to be explored simulataneously but it also uses quantum fourier transform. Which is used to 
      analyse the frequency components of a signal. These both make it faster and more efficient to classical alogrithims (Monz et al., 2016).

Analysis

      Grover's and Shor's algorithms offer promising advantages in targeting and prediction within financial markets because of their quantum properties, 
      which gives them quadratic and exponential speed ups compared to classical algorithms (Wicaksana, A., Anthony, A., & Wicaksono, A. W. (2020)).
      Grover's algorithm achieves quadratic speedup by using quantum superposition and amplitude amplification, allowing it to search unsorted databases in O(SQRT(N)) time, compared to the classical O(N).
      Shor's algorithm achieves exponential speedup by using quantum parallelism and the quantum Fourier transform to factor large numbers in polynomial time O ((log N) ^3), compared to the exponential time of classical methods
      It is also presented with challenges of their own.One of the biggest challenges that Grover’s algorithm has is that it is limited by the current quantum hardware limitations as quantum computing is a recently 
      new field of computing with a lot of nuances to be had. Another disadvantage it could run into is the integration within classical systems as most current institutions 
      rely on these classical systems. Also, as dataset of financial markets grow Grover’s algorithm might not always be a practical advantage if the data is not structured correctly. 
      Shor’s algorithm similar disadvantages to Grover’s but it also has specificity issues. Particularly in targeting and prediction. The algorithm is primarily focused 
      on factoring making its utility within financial context more limited and specific compared to Grover’s. Furthermore, one of Shor’s strengths for efficiently 
      tackling large factoring efficiently poses as a huge threat to already existing cryptographic methods leading to massive security risks and greater need for financial 
      institution to implement new cryptographic methods. (Graph in excel)
